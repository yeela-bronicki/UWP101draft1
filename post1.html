Introduction

About the Blog

As I reflected on my coursework and research while working on this blog, I realized that design disciplines are interwoven yet unique. Having such a broad variety of courses at UC Davis has been instrumental in my career-finding journey and in putting together this project. I selected the topic of design careers, as influenced by my personal experiences- which will be discussed in the other section of this introductory post and the rest of the blog. I will be presenting information about three design disciplines that I have experience with and seem to be promising careers.
I chose to write about graphic design, UI/UX, and web design and how these disciplines connect to a post-graduation career due to their popularity within the design program and my previous experience with courses. For any new design students reading this, here are some very brief one-sentence descriptions of these kinds of design:

  • Graphic design sends messages through visual communication.
  • UI/UX refers to user interface and user experience design, or the ways a design product looks (UI) and how a user will interact with it (UX).
  • Web design plans and arranges content on a webpage that allows it to be shared.

Throughout this blog, I will combine knowledge from my personal experience in UC Davis design courses and secondary research. I will highlight connections between these disciplines, especially as it relates to coursework offered at UC Davis in the design department in order to create a more comprehensive look at design possibilities.

About the Author

Hello, my name is Ye'ela Bronicki. As of writing this, I am a Design major at UC Davis, graduating in June 2025. I was inspired by my experience as a student to create this blog in order to reflect on my steps toward a design career while providing insight to my design peers. During my time as a student at UC Davis, I have taken a variety of courses. They range from sustainable design to the history of interior architecture, and photography to coding. As the program at UC Davis is so broad, I found that it can be overwhelming to figure out which classes I am most interested in. My goal for this blog is to make the course selection process easier for my peers and help them (and me) determine what options feel most interesting.
As I am working towards finding a job post-graduation, I chose to code and design this blog site. I used my HTML (HyperText Markup Language, a coding language) and CSS (Cascading Style Sheets, a design programming language) skills to create a responsive layout for desktop and mobile devices and created the visuals for each post using my knowledge and past projects as applicable. I selected graphic design, web design, and UI/UX to write about within the time frame of the course since I realized connections between them prior to my research (through my coursework) and they are popular career choices that I think remain somewhat mysterious to college students.
